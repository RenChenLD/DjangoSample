Natural language processing

Maximum entropy tagger:
In natural language processing tasks, Part-Of-Speech tagging is one of the most important text analysis tasks used to classify words into their part-of-speech and label them according the tag set which is a collection of tags used for the POS tagging.
After implemented the Maximum Entropy Tagger, I did some experiments to evaluate the performance comparing with a back-off tagger. The back-off tagger is consisted of Unigram Tagger, Bigram Tagger, and Trigram Tagger. The data corpus used for the experiments are Treebank, Conll2000, and Brown. The taggers were tested in different sizes of the corpuses.
The conclusion is that the Maximum Entropy Tagger has a better performance than Back-Off Tagger on accuracy while the Maximum Entropy Tagger takes more time to finish tasks.
